The Perfect Software Solution To Transcribe Digitized Collection Content

With our custom demo of the software we show you how FromThePage can be configured to accomplish your specific goals with your actual materials. One way we do this is by sharing case studies of other users to show how our features can support your transcription needs in a variety of ways.

The following features are especially relevant and utilized by digital humanities faculty and librarians, researchers, and instructors:

  • Collaborative Transcription & Translation.  Support your digital humanities projects, from teaching with primary sources to assisting digital scholarly research.

  • Version Control & Track Changes.  Tools to oversee, track, control, and review projects so you can guide transcriptions for quality control; important for open scholarship and grading in classroom use.

  • Collaboration.  Page level comments and discussion create a rich experience for students and instructors and aid research groups in collaborating efficiently.

  • Private Projects.  Project owners can specify specific access to materials. Used to restrict editing to members of a research team or to a classroom group.

  • Indexing & Annotation.  Users may tag subjects like toponyms or personal names, automatically creating an index in the text, preserving variant phrases or spellings used to refer to the same subject.

  • Deep Zoom.  Collaborators can zoom to decipher difficult handwriting on uploaded documents.

  • Configurable Fields.  Field-based transcription to support pre-printed forms allows transcription of structured data from index cards, government forms, spreadsheets, and questionnaires.

  • Metadata Creation Via Custom Forms.  Standardized metadata fields can be created so an index of related documents can easily be searched in the future.

  • Integration Via IIIF & CONTENTdm.  Import options to load collections and associated metadata as easily as entering URLs.
