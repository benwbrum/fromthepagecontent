The Perfect Software Solution To Turn Digitized Collection Content Into Findable Materials

With our custom demo of the software we show you how FromThePage can be configured to accomplish your specific goals with your actual materials. One way we do this is by sharing case studies of other users to show how our features can support your transcription needs in a variety of ways.

The following features are especially relevant and utilized by librarians and archivists:

  • Crowdsourced & Collaborative Transcription & Translation.  Support your programming with an online component of public engagement of transcription and translation of primary source materials.

  • Configurable Fields.  Field-based transcription to support pre-printed forms allows transcription of structured data from index cards, government forms, spreadsheets, and questionnaires.

  • OCR Correction.  Review of an OCR text compared to the original source document allows for human corrected transcriptions.

  • Metadata Creation Via Custom Forms.  Standardized metadata fields can be created so an index of related documents can easily be searched in the future.

  • Deep Zoom.  Collaborators can zoom to decipher difficult handwriting on uploaded documents.

  • Collaboration.  Page level comments and discussion create a rich experience for transcribers to become more knowledgeable and precise over-time.

  • Version Control & Track Changes.  Tools to oversee, track, control, and review projects so you can guide transcriptions for quality control.

  • Private Projects.  Project owners can grant access to specific people only if they wish. Used to restrict editing to members of a small research team or to provide control over the project materials to members of indigenous communities.

  • Integration Via IIIF & CONTENTdm.  Import options to load collections and associated metadata as simply as entering URLs.

  • Indexing & Annotation. Users may tag subjects like toponyms or personal names, automatically creating an index in the text, preserving variant phrases or spellings used to refer to the same subject.
